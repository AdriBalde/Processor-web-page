Other processor types

So far we have referred to processor or CPU interchangeably, However, they are not the same from a technical point of view.

Technically, a processor is an integrated electronic circuit that performs logical, arithmetic and input/output operations. Under this definition the CPU is only one of the processors in a computer. The CPU is the processor that is dedicated to all general tasks that do not have a specialized processor. Another processor present in all computers is the GPU (Graphics Processing Unit).

Graphics Processing Unit (GPU)

A GPU is a parallel processor that allows the repetitive calculations of painting the screen within an application and all other processes to run at the same time. GPUs were introduced at the end of the last century to help CPUs keep pace, since at the CPUs had a hard time calculating graphics and this caused lag problems. Thus was created the GPU, a specialized graphics processor that allows the fluidity and amount of animations we have today.

Currently we can distinguish mainly two types of GPUs: those that are integrated in the same chip as the CPU, which are cheaper and less powerful, and the "external" ones. The latter are a separate component of the computer that connects to the processor through buses on the motherboard, while the integrated GPUs are connected to the CPU internally within the chip. External GPUs are more powerful and are the ones used in artificial intelligence training and execution, in video and image editing at a professional level, in computers prepared for video games, even in computers prepared for mining cryptocurrencies...

However, this distinction is starting to become less clear with the release of Apple's M1 Pro and M1 Max processors, which have graphics integrated into the same chip as the CPU and are still capable of performing the same tasks and with the same efficiency as the most powerful external graphics on the market.

Application-Specific Instruction-set Processor (ASIP)

ASIP processors are those that have been created to perform a single function. Their circuitry is specially designed for a single type of calculations. For example, to mine BitCoins there have been created processors that are the fastest at solving the problem required to mine a BitCoin. There are also processors for audio processing that can be found in chips of electrical instruments such as keyboards, amplifiers, mixing consoles...

Physics Processing Unit (PPU)

A PPU is a microprocessor designed for physics computation, especially for the physics engine of a video game.

These processors arose with the idea of having specialized processors that perform operations that cost a lot for the CPU and thereby reduce its load, it is similar to the idea behind GPUs, which perform the graphics which perform the graphics calculations so that the CPU does not have to do them.

Digital Signal Processor (DSP)

A DSP is a specialized microprocessor whose architecture is optimized for the needs of digital signal processing. They are widely used in audio signal processing, telecommunications, digital image processing, radar, sonar and voice recognition systems, and in devices we use every day such as cell phones, hard drives and high-definition televisions.

Their function is typically to measure, filter or compress real-world continuous analog signals. General purpose processors are also capable of executing digital signal processing algorithms successfully, but they are often not able to keep up with real-time processing. In addition, DSPs usually have better power efficiency, so they are better for mobile devices because power consumption is important for longer battery life.

Quantum computers

Quantum computers are a new paradigm at both the software and hardware levels. These computers do not use processors like the ones we have seen so far, they take advantage of the quantum properties of particles and instead of bits they use qubits (quantum bits).
